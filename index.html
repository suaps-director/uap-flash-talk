It’s All About Evidence — But What Is the Evidence?

Michael Cifone, PhD

For decades, UAP research has leaned on a forensic model: witness reports, radar returns, fragment analysis. But this forensic approach is backward-looking, post-facto, and methodologically vague.

Shifting Toward Observational Science

Emerging initiatives like the Galileo Project point toward a science of direct observation— instrumented, systematic, and repeatable. This mirrors how investigative medicine treats anomalous cases: not as evidence in themselves, but as guides to structure new data-gathering efforts.

Existential Empiricism

Drawing from Spinoza, William James, and Deleuze, I argue for a philosophical shift: lived experience should be treated as epistemically meaningful. Some UAP data resists reduction— not due to noise, but because it exceeds current scientific categories.

Recommendations

  • Deploy multi-modal sensor arrays
  • Support metadata standards for openness and interoperability
  • Bridge NLP and qualitative analysis of UAP witness records
  • Maintain archives of gray literature and observational data
  • Support interdisciplinary and conceptual research
